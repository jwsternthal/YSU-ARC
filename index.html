The Amateur Radio Club at Youngstown State University

About

We are the Amateur Radio Club here at Youngstown State University. K8YSU, the station of the YSU Amateur Radio Club, is active on HF (high frequency) "Short-Wave" and VHF (very high frequency) bands. Club members have contacted other amateur radio operators "hams" in over 50 countries since the station went on the air in January 2001 through a very generous donation by Norm Into, K8NI.

We are equipped for voice, Morse and computer data communications. When an improved antenna array is available, we will be able to use amateur satellites. YSUARC is looking for student members. The only requirement is an interest in radio communication. Training for the FCC license is available and of course, already licensed amateur operators are welcome.

In addition to technical work on the equipment and on the air operation of K8YSU, special events such as simulated emergency operation in the field are planned. If you are interested, contact the faculty or technical advisors of the YSU/ARC: Gordon Frissora (FA); WB8YHD, Frank Sole (FA); KC8RSH, Ron Krauss (TA); or the license trustee, WB8YHD, Frank Sole (LT).
